Experiment 02 · Automotive Sensor

Reverse Collision Detection via Bumper PZT Sensor

PZT-5H disc on rear bumper detects collision vibration when reversing. F = ½mv²/d from car mass and speed; charge Q = d₃₃ × F triggers LED and brake signal instantly.

PZT-5H bumper mount 800–2500 kg · 1–10 m/s F=½mv²/d · Q=d₃₃F LED + Brake signal

🎯 Aim

Detect rear-end collision while reversing using bumper PZT sensor, and activate alert circuit at or before moment of impact.

🧪 Materials

  • PZT-5H on rear bumper
  • Op-Amp unity-gain buffer
  • 100nF charge amplifier
  • 220Ω resistor
  • Arduino Uno comparator
  • Red LED + 5V buzzer

⚡ Physics

Kinetic energy ½mv² → impulse F = ½mv²/d. PZT stress generates Q = d₃₃ × F. At 2m/s, 1200kg: F≈48,000N → Q≈28.4μC → V>5V (ADC saturates = guaranteed trigger).

🔑 Key Variables

  • m — vehicle mass (kg)
  • v — reverse speed (m/s)
  • d — crumple distance (m)
  • d₃₃ — PZT coeff (pC/N)

📊 Results

  • Far: Sensor idle
  • Near: Beep dots, rising signal
  • Impact: LED ON + brake signal
  • 2m/s+1200kg → Q≈28.4μC

⚠️ Fail-Safe

Sensor is completely passive. Zero power needed for detection. Only LED/buzzer needs 5V supply. Power loss = still detects, just no alarm sound.

📋 Step-by-Step Process
01
Car Reverses

Vehicle moves in reverse at speed v (1–10 m/s). Proximity bar fills as gap closes toward the parked vehicle.

02
Proximity Warning

Below 3m, arc lines pulse on bumper. Beep frequency increases. System status changes to "DETECTING → NEAR → IMPACT IMMINENT".

03
Collision — F = ½mv²/d

v=2m/s, m=1200kg, d=0.05m → F = ½×1200×4÷0.05 = 48,000 N. Skid marks and screen shake simulate real impact physics.

04
Q = d₃₃ × F → V = Q/C

Q = 593 × 48,000 = 28.4 μC. V = 28.4μC ÷ 100nF = 284V (clamped to 5V by op-amp). ADC guaranteed to saturate.

05
Circuit Fires — LED + Brake Signal

Red alert LED blazes on the PCB circuit below. Buzzer rings. In real vehicle, a relay triggers emergency braking.

📝 Conclusion

Bumper-mounted PZT reliably detects even low-speed collisions. Even at 1 m/s the generated voltage (71V) far exceeds the 5V ADC range — guaranteeing trigger. Proves piezoelectric sensing as a passive, fail-safe, zero-power detection method ideal for automotive safety.
