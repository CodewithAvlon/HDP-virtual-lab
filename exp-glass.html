Glass Break & Window Vibration Detection

A PZT-5H disc fixed to a glass pane converts mechanical shock from impact directly into charge Q = d₃₃ × F. After Op-Amp conditioning, the Arduino triggers an alarm LED and buzzer when force exceeds the threshold.

PZT-5H · d₃₃=593 pC/N Trigger: F > 150 N 1–5 kHz range Op-Amp buffer + comparator Red LED + Buzzer output

🎯 Aim

Detect glass break vibration using a PZT sensor and trigger an alarm — demonstrating the direct piezoelectric effect in a real anti-theft application.

🧪 Materials

  • PZT-5H disc (27mm)
  • Window glass panel
  • Op-Amp LM358 buffer
  • 100nF cap, 220Ω resistor
  • Arduino Uno (ADC)
  • Red LED + 5V buzzer

⚡ Physics

Direct piezoelectric effect: stress on PZT lattice displaces ion pairs, creating net dipole moment. Charge Q = d₃₃ × F is generated proportional to applied force F.

📊 Expected Results

  • Safe (<150N) → No alarm
  • Cracked (150–300N) → Standby
  • Shattered (>300N) → LED ON
  • At 300N: Q=177,900 pC, V≈1.78V
📋 Step-by-Step Process
01
Object Strikes Glass

Force F hits the glass pane. Shock wave propagates to the PZT disc. Slider range: 10N (tap) → 500N (brick).

02
PZT Generates Charge: Q = d₃₃ × F

At 300N: Q = 593 × 300 = 177,900 pC. Crystal lattice deforms, separating dipole charges. This is the raw sensor output before any amplification.

03
Op-Amp Converts to Voltage: V = Q/C

100nF cap: V = 177,900 pC ÷ 100nF = 1.779V. Unity-gain buffer preserves signal integrity without loading the sensor.

04
Arduino ADC Compares to Threshold

If V > V_threshold (set in Calculators), digital output goes HIGH, eliminating false alarms from minor vibrations.

05
LED Lights & Buzzer Sounds

Red LED on PCB circuit illuminates and pulses. Buzzer activates. Real system also sends SMS via GSM module.

06
System Resets

Capacitor discharges to baseline. Alarm latches until manually reset. System ready for next event immediately.

📝 Conclusion

PZT-5H successfully converts glass-break vibration into an electrical signal. Q = d₃₃ × F scales linearly with force, and the Op-Amp stage conditions it for reliable Arduino detection. This demonstrates the direct piezoelectric effect — sensor requires zero power to detect, only the alarm output needs supply.
