Introduction to
Piezoelectricity

⚡

Direct Effect

When mechanical stress is applied to a piezoelectric material, it generates an electric charge proportional to the applied force. This is the direct piezoelectric effect.

🔄

Converse Effect

An applied electric field induces mechanical deformation in the material. Enables actuators, ultrasonic transducers, and precision positioning.

📡

Transducer Role

A PZT sensor converts mechanical energy (vibration, pressure, acceleration) directly into a measurable AC electrical voltage — no external power required.

🧪

Key Materials

Natural: Quartz, Tourmaline
Ceramics: PZT-5H (high sensitivity)
Polymers: PVDF (flexible films)

Animation

Piezoelectric Effect Visualizer

Crystal Lattice – The animated lattice shows ion displacement under stress. Positive and negative ions shift, creating a net dipole moment.

Compression Wave – Yellow arrows represent the applied mechanical stress cycling in and out at the set vibration frequency.

Electric Field – Purple field lines emerge from the surface charge separation, driving current in the external circuit.

Charge Output – The charge Q = d · F, where d is the piezoelectric coefficient (pC/N) and F is applied force (N).
